About Me

I specialize in bacteriology and space life science, focusing on bacterial pathogenesis, genetic engineering, biofilms, space biology, vaccine development, and host-pathogen interaction. My work leverages diverse human surrogate models, such as 3D tissue cultures and animal models, with a goal of advancing our understanding of microbial interactions within ecosystems. This research aims to uncover microbial community dynamics, persistence, and pathogenicity both on Earth and in space, supporting life sustainability in challenging environments.

I hold an Alfred P. Sloan Postdoctoral Fellowship for studying the ISS microbiome, with publications on genomics, microbiology, and space biology. I currently lead a NASA-funded project on bacterial interspecies interactions in extreme environments, integrating systemic approaches and multi-directional analysis to explore complex microbial interactions. My achievements include the ASM Peggy Cotter Early Career Award and an Outstanding Teaching Award.
